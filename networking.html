Building your professional network

Building your professional network is one of the most valuable steps you can take toward advancing your career. Networking isn’t just about meeting people—it’s about creating authentic connections, learning from others, and opening doors to new opportunities. Whether you’re preparing for your first informational interview or looking to strengthen relationships in your field, these resources will guide you along the way.

UMSI students have access to an incredible community of alumni, peers, faculty, and industry partners. With the right strategies and tools, you can build meaningful connections that support your career journey now and long into the future.

Ways to Build Your Network

  • Tap into the UMSI community. Reach out to alumni through LinkedIn or the alumni directory.
  • Engage in professional associations. Many groups host student memberships and events that can connect you with practitioners in your field.
  • Attend events and panels. Career fairs, information sessions, and workshops are opportunities to meet professionals in a low-pressure setting.
  • Practice informational interviews. These conversations allow you to learn about different career paths and gather advice directly from people in the field.

Resume FAQ

  • How long should my resume be?
    For most UMSI students and new grads, one page is best. More experienced professionals may require two pages.
  • Do I need a different resume for each application?
    It’s wise to tailor your resume to the requirements and keywords of each opportunity.
  • Is it okay to include coursework or student projects?
    Absolutely! UMSI coursework and projects are valued by employers, especially if they show relevant skills and impact.
  • How often should I update my resume?
    Update your resume every semester, after major projects, or whenever you gain a new role or responsibility.
